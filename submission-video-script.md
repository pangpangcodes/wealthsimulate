# Demo Video Script (~2:45)

Screen-record the app while narrating. Keep it moving - cut dead air, don't wait for loading.

---

## 0:00 - 0:25 | The problem (25s)

**Screen:** Blank or a static financial plan PDF (optional, just for visual contrast).

**Say:**
"Financial planning today is either expensive or broken. You either pay a wealth manager thousands a year for a static PDF that shows one forecast, or you open a spreadsheet and guess at a single rate of return. Neither shows you the range of what could happen. I rebuilt this workflow from scratch as an AI-native system."

---

## 0:25 - 0:45 | Onboarding (20s)

**Screen:** Upload a Wealthsimple statement. Show Claude vision extracting accounts and holdings.

**Say:**
"You start by uploading a Wealthsimple statement. Claude vision extracts your accounts, holdings, and balances - no manual data entry. It picks up account types, asset classes, and builds your financial profile automatically."

**Action:** Skip through the review wizard quickly (don't dwell on each step). Land on the dashboard.

---

## 0:45 - 1:00 | The dashboard (15s)

**Screen:** Dashboard with the net worth fan chart, metric cards, and the blue info card (verdict).

**Say:**
"The dashboard shows your current trajectory across 1,000 simulated futures - worst case, most likely, best case. Not one number - a range. The system proactively flags what it noticed: portfolio concentration, tax opportunities, and where you stand."

---

## 1:00 - 1:45 | Ask a what-if question (45s)

**Screen:** Open the chat. Type: "What if I buy a home in 2028 for $500K with 15% down?"

**Say:**
"Now the interesting part. You ask questions in plain English. The AI interprets this into a structured simulation - it calculates the down payment drawdown, builds a 25-year mortgage, and models the opportunity cost of pulling money from your portfolio."

**Action:** Show the Phase 1 response (brief, no numbers). Then the simulation runs, the chart updates with a new scenario line, and Phase 2 streams in with real numbers.

**Say:**
"It runs 1,000 paths with correlated asset returns and real Canadian tax rules - federal plus provincial brackets, RRSP and TFSA contribution logic, CPP and OAS at 65. Then it explains what the numbers actually mean for your life."

---

## 1:45 - 2:05 | Stack a second scenario (20s)

**Screen:** Type: "What if I also lose my job next year for 6 months?"

**Say:**
"You can stack scenarios. Now we're testing a home purchase combined with job loss. The AI runs near-term cash flow analysis - how many months of runway your liquid accounts cover, what the monthly shortfall looks like, and recovery options."

**Action:** Show the updated chart with both scenarios, the metric cards showing deltas.

---

## 2:05 - 2:30 | Where AI stops (25s)

**Screen:** Point to the response where it says "the simulation shows" (never "you should"). Point to the "book an advisor" link.

**Say:**
"Here's the deliberate boundary. The AI never says 'you should' or 'I recommend.' It says 'the simulation shows.' And it can't fabricate numbers - the two-phase architecture means Claude interprets your question first, then the deterministic engine runs the math separately. The AI only sees results after they actually exist. It's architecturally impossible for it to hallucinate a financial figure."

**Say:**
"The decision that must stay human: whether to actually buy the home, change your savings rate, or retire early. Risk tolerance is personal. The AI shows what could happen across 1,000 futures. You decide what matters."

---

## 2:30 - 2:45 | Close (15s)

**Screen:** Dashboard with multiple scenarios visible.

**Say:**
"A 33-year-old Canadian with $25K saved can now explore 50 financial scenarios in an afternoon and understand the range of outcomes. That used to require a wealth manager. This is what financial planning looks like when you rebuild it with AI at the centre instead of layering it on top."

---

## Recording tips

- Record at 1920x1080, browser in a clean window (hide bookmarks bar, other tabs)
- Use example profile (Alex, 33) so the numbers are consistent
- Pre-type the questions if you want clean delivery, or type live for authenticity
- Cut aggressively - trim loading time, don't show the full streaming response
- Total should land at 2:30 to 2:50
- No background music needed - clean narration is better
